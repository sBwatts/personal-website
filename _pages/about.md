I examine how emerging tools — from AI-assisted body-worn cameras to co-response models — reshape police behavior, officer decision-making, and community outcomes. My work sits at the intersection of criminology, public health, and applied policy evaluation.

My research centers on three interconnected areas. First, I study how emerging technologies reshape day-to-day police practice. A significant portion of this work examines **body-worn cameras**: how automated AI-assisted review changes supervisor behavior, officer accountability, and officer acceptance of monitoring systems.

Second, I work on **de-escalation training** — what it is, what works, and why. This line of research has direct policy relevance as departments face pressure to reduce use-of-force incidents and improve officer-community relationships.

Third, my public health work examines **police responses to opioid overdoses and co-response models**, focusing on how structural arrangements between law enforcement and public health agencies shape outcomes for individuals in crisis.
